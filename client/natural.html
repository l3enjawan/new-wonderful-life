Khao Sok National Park

Khao Sok is perfectly situated on the mainland between Phuket, Krabi, Khao Lak and Koh Samui, the most popular destinations in Southern Thailand. It is a fantastic place to go on vacation. Close and personal elephant encounters, jungle trekking on foot and canoeing are all possible activities, which will give you the experience of a lifetime.

Rajjaprabha Dam

Ratchaprapha Dam was originally called Cheow Lan Dam. It is the second multi-purpose dam in the south, and it is located in Surat Thani. When the building was constructed, His Majesty the King bestowed a royal blessing on it. It was given a new name by the King, "Ratchaprapha Dam" means "Light of the Reign"

Wat Phra Borommathat Chaiya

Wat Phra Borommathat Chaiya Ratchaworawihan is located in Tambon Wiang. The pagoda here is an ancient monument built with the influence of the Mahayana sect of Buddhism in the Srivijaya period. The pagoda is surrounded by small pagodas in the four cardinal directions, Wat Phra Borommathat Chaiya Ratchaworawihan and a cloister where ancient Buddha images in different sizes are housed goes around the four sides. The pagoda is a revered monument of importance in Buddhism of Surat Thani.

Khlong Nam Sai

“Khlong Nam Sai” was named after its physical feature as the Canal’s water is so clear that people can see the ground and fish. This canal is a natural pure watershed that runs down for the livelihood of the people in the communities in Amphoe Khiri Rat Nikhom.
